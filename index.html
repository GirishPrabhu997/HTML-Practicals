Veg Biriyani Recipe

Ingredients (Specifications)

  1. 2 cups basmati rice, rinsed and soaked 20–30 minutes
  2. 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrot, peas, beans, potato) chopped
  3. 2 medium onions, thinly sliced
  4. 2 tomatoes, chopped
  5. 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  6. 2 green chilies, slit (optional)
  7. 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  8. 3 tbsp oil or ghee
  9. 2 tbsp biryani masala
  10. 1 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  11. 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  12. 1/2 tsp garam masala
  13. Salt to taste
  14. Whole spices: 1 bay leaf, 4 cloves, 4 green cardamom, 1" cinnamon stick, 1 tsp cumin seeds
  15. 4 cups water (for rice) or as needed
  16. Fresh coriander and mint, chopped (1/4–1/2 cup each)
  17. Optional: a pinch of saffron soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Rinse and soak the basmati rice for 20–30 minutes. Drain before cooking.
  2. Bring 4 cups water to a boil with 1 tsp salt and the whole spices. Add rice and cook until 70–80% done (grains should hold shape). Drain and set aside.
  3. Heat oil/ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan. Fry sliced onions until deep golden brown. Remove half for garnish and keep the rest in the pan.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies; sauté 1 minute until fragrant.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes; cook until they soften and oil separates.
  6. Stir in turmeric, red chili powder, biryani masala, garam masala and salt. Cook 1–2 minutes.
  7. Add mixed vegetables and a splash of water; cover and cook until vegetables are partially cooked (about 5–7 minutes).
  8. Lower heat and fold in yogurt and half the chopped coriander and mint. Adjust seasoning.
  9. Layering (Dum): In the pan with vegetables, spread half the par-cooked rice, sprinkle some fried onions, herbs, and a little ghee/saffron milk. Repeat with remaining rice and toppings.
  10. Cover tightly with a lid (seal edges with dough if available) and cook on very low heat for 15–25 minutes (dum) until rice and vegetables finish cooking and flavors meld.
  11. Turn off heat and rest 10 minutes. Gently fluff the biryani with a fork, mixing layers lightly.
  12. Serve hot with raita, salad, or boiled eggs (optional). Garnish with reserved fried onions and fresh herbs.
